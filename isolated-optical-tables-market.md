<p><p>Isolated optical tables are critical for minimizing vibrations in precision optical experiments. Two primary types exist: elastomerically isolated optical tables and pneumatically isolated optical tables. </p><p>Elastomerically isolated tables use rubber-like materials to absorb vibrations, offering high stability and ease of setup, making them suitable for various laboratory environments. In contrast, pneumatically isolated tables utilize air pressure systems to provide superior isolation from external disturbances, ideal for high-performance applications requiring exceptional stability and low-frequency vibration control.</p></p>
