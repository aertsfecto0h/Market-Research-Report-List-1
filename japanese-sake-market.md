<p><p>The Japanese sake market consists of various types categorized by brewing methods and ingredient quality. Ordinary sake is widely consumed and affordable, while Junmai is made solely from rice, water, and koji, offering a richer flavor. Honjozo includes a small amount of distilled alcohol, enhancing aroma and smoothness. Junmai Ginjo and Ginjo are premium categories, crafted from highly polished rice, resulting in refined, aromatic profiles, with Junmai Ginjo being pure and Ginjo containing a touch of distilled alcohol for added complexity.</p></p>
