<p><p>The influenza vaccination market comprises three main types: egg-based, cell culture-based, and recombinant vaccines. Egg-based vaccines, the traditional method, are produced by growing the virus in chicken eggs. Cell culture vaccines utilize mammalian cell lines, offering faster production and enhanced safety. Recombinant vaccines are engineered using genetic technology, allowing for quicker development and targeted immune responses. Each type effectively helps mitigate influenza outbreaks, catering to diverse production needs and regulatory requirements in public health.</p></p>
