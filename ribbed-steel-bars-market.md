<p><p>The ribbed steel bars market can be segmented into hot-rolled and cold-rolled types. Hot-rolling involves heating steel above its recrystallization temperature, resulting in bars with enhanced ductility and strength, commonly used in construction and infrastructure. In contrast, cold-rolling involves processing steel at room temperature, producing bars with tighter tolerances and improved surface finish, suitable for applications requiring precision. Each type caters to specific industry needs, influencing market demand and pricing dynamics based on performance characteristics.</p></p>
