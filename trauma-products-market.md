<p><p>The trauma products market is primarily divided into internal and external fixators, both crucial for stabilizing fractures. Internal fixators involve devices like plates and screws surgically implanted within the body to hold bones in place during healing. Conversely, external fixators are external frames attached to the affected limb that stabilize fractures from outside the body. Both types cater to various orthopedic needs, enhancing recovery outcomes and ensuring the proper alignment of fractured bones during the healing process.</p></p>
